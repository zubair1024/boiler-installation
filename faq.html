Understanding Your Boiler

System boilers heat your radiators directly and they usually store hot water in a storage cylinder. These are commonly located in either an airing cupboard or in a garage. They heat a large volume of water at once and then store this hot water until you decide to use it. They are less efficient than combi boilers, which only heat the water you need, when you need it.

Also known as conventional boiler, the standard boiler stores hot water in a storage cylinder, similar to system boilers, but the difference is that a standard boiler is fed by a separate coldwater tank – commonly found in the loft. There's another separate tank, called a ‘feed and expansion cistern', usually in the loft too. Standard boilers are the largest type of boiler and so are not generally suitable for smaller living spaces.

The ‘ERP’ – Energy related products label will tell you how efficiently your boiler (or any other appliance like a fridge or a washing machine) uses energy. It's designed to help you choose the right appliance and is designed to ensure you save the most money on your heating and energy bills. The ERP label will rate an appliance from A+++ to G.

A+++ is the most efficient boiler you can get.

It also tells you how loud the appliance is in decibels and how much heat it outputs in kilowatts.

All of the boilers available to you on the 24|7 Staywarm packages are rated A for efficiency.

Combi boilers, or ‘combination boilers’ as they are known are the most popular type of boiler in the UK. In fact, they’re so popular that they’re used by 74% of households across the country!

They're named ‘combination' because they combine both a high-efficiency water heater and a central heating boiler all in one simple package and they are highly efficient. Also, combi boilers can save you lots of money on your heating bills so they’re a great option for those looking to save some money.

That's because:

  • They heat the water only when you need it, rather than storing hot water up like traditional boilers
  • They're small and compact, as they don't use an external hot water tank so are great for those living in apartments or for those with limited space.

Thermostatic radiator valves – or TRVs as they’re most commonly known – are controls for your radiators. They regulate how much hot water flows into the radiator so you can adjust its temperature. If you’d rather have it hotter in one room than another, simply alter the dial accordingly.

TRVs also let you turn some radiators off (for Nine, in a room you're not using) without completely turning the heating off.

Why do I need TRVs?

The Government recommends that every household has thermostatic radiator valves because they:

  • Save you money on heating bills
  • Use less energy
  • Are more energy efficient and are better for the environmen

But they're not mandatory, so if you don’t have them don’t worry too much. TRV’s simply give you more control over your home heating.

A flue is a pipe, almost like a chimney, that carries waste from the boiler to the outside. You can see your flue exit (the part of the flue that comes out of your house) on the outside wall or roof of your home.

Flues can either be round or square depending on the home that you live in.

If the flue is less than two metres off the ground, or if it’s underneath a car park or shelter, it'll be covered by a guard. It will include a piece of wire mesh that surrounds the flue.
